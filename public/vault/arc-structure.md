# 📐 Arc Structure — Halden City

> The four-arc philosophical progression for Legacy of Halden City.

---

## The Through-Line

Each arc forces the next logical question:

| Arc | Central Question |
|-----|-----------------|
| **Arc 1** | Should these institutions exist? |
| **Arc 2** | What fills the vacuum when they collapse? |
| **Arc 3** | Does Earth deserve to survive without its guarantor? |
| **Arc 4** | Who has the right to build what comes next — and does the act of deciding corrupt them? |

---

## Arc 1 — Institutions Questioned *(Active)*

**Antagonist:** Signal
**Question:** *Should these institutions exist?*

Signal's diagnosis — that the Paragon/Renegade Force cycle and A.E.G.I.S.'s institutional power are inherited structures no one consented to — is not entirely wrong. That's what makes him dangerous. His methods are the problem.

**End state:** Signal engineers the removal of both cosmic Forces. The Alliance fractures. Instability begins. Signal escapes — the arc does not resolve him.

---

## Arc 2 — Life Without the Old Institutions

**Question:** *What fills the vacuum when they collapse?*

The answer is fear. A.E.G.I.S., freed from the Alliance's moral check and the Forces' deterrence, turns on the enhanced individuals it can no longer control. Heroes go underground, get captured, or get compromised. The institutional betrayal arc.

---

## Arc 3 — The Auditors *(Short Arc)*

**Antagonist:** The Auditors
**Question:** *Does Earth deserve to survive without its guarantor?*

Kiara defected because Paragon convinced her life was worth protecting. Now Paragon is gone. The Auditors' original assessment becomes defensible again. This arc is load-bearing for Starfall — she has to prove the conviction without Paragon behind it.

**End state:** Auditors fail or retreat. How and why they retreat seeds Arc 4's antagonist — someone who watches humanity survive and draws the wrong conclusion about what that means.

**Darkstar is the prime Arc 4 candidate:** He's been stress-testing humanity for 13 years. His thesis — suffering accelerates evolution — positions him perfectly for "the one who watched and decided he should build what comes next."

---

## Arc 4 — The New System

**Question:** *Who has the right to build what comes next — and does the act of deciding corrupt them?*

A villain whose diagnosis of the problem is correct. The heroes cannot argue the villain is wrong about the past — only about who gets to determine the future, and whether any single will should have that authority.

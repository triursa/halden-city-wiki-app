## What They Are

The Wavelengths are an ancient interdimensional species that exist as pure electromagnetic energy at the extremes of the light spectrum. They are not invaders from another dimension — they exist *in* this one, in the substrate beneath visible reality, in frequencies that have always been present but have never been perceived as minded. They predate recorded history. They predate humanity. They are genuinely alien in the oldest sense: not malevolent, not benevolent, operating according to a logic that has no overlap with human moral categories.

They exist in two factions:

**High Spectrum** — ultraviolet, X-ray, gamma. The burning end. Revelation, exposure, the light that kills. High-spectrum hosts trend toward illumination — the drive to expose what is hidden, to force truth into the open, to burn away what cannot withstand scrutiny.

**Low Spectrum** — infrared, radio, the long invisible warmth. Patience and influence. The signal beneath the noise. Low-spectrum hosts trend toward shadow — the belief that spectacle is a lie, that what matters operates unseen, that darkness is not absence but presence of a different kind.

Neither faction is "good." Neither is conscious in a way that maps cleanly onto human intentionality. They are ancient, patient, and entirely indifferent to human welfare except where it intersects with their purposes.

---

## How They Operate

The Wavelengths cannot maintain stable presence in the physical world without organic hosts. Their energy is too concentrated, too alien, to exist in matter without a biological anchor. For millennia they have seeded hosts opportunistically — anywhere that extreme electromagnetic energy concentrates, they can exert enough will to bond with a human caught at the center of it. Industrial accidents, weapons tests, laboratory explosions. They do not cause these events. They use them.

**The host mechanic:**

A bonded host carries one faction's energy as power. The bond is not a possession — the host remains themselves. But over time, the host's existing worldview is *amplified* in the direction of their faction's nature. The Wavelengths find the frequency already present in the human and turn up the volume. Hosts believe their convictions are their own. Mostly they are. The Wavelengths just selected for them.

**Same-spectrum hosts are rivals.** Two high-spectrum hosts or two low-spectrum hosts in proximity are drawn into conflict. The Wavelengths' ecology is designed so the stronger absorbs the weaker, producing a more powerful host and a stronger faction foothold. This is not commanded. It is instinct, cultivated over years of subtle amplification.

**Opposite-spectrum hosts are complementary.** Like magnetic poles, a high-spectrum and low-spectrum host are drawn together. Paired, they are more stable, more powerful, and more useful to both factions simultaneously. The natural endgame the Wavelengths design toward is a bonded high/low pair — a dual anchor in the physical world that serves both factions' presence together.

A paired set is the system working as intended.

## The Glass District Explosion (2022)

The experiment was an electromagnetic test — high-density radiation at frequencies far beyond safe parameters, conducted inside a Glass District research facility. The Wavelengths did not engineer it. But the energy concentration was sufficient for both factions to simultaneously exert influence, and they did.

Jason James bonded to High Spectrum. Mary Matthews bonded to Low Spectrum. Both from the same origin event, at the same moment, while still in contact with each other.

The Wavelengths, working in parallel through the same explosion, produced a natural opposite-spectrum pair. Jason and Artemis were drawn to each other from the start because the system designed them to be. Their partnership felt cosmically significant because it was — just not in the way either of them understood.

---

## What Happened to Jason

Morgan Vale (Nightfall) is a low-spectrum host who manifested separately, in 2022, through an Eclipse-adjacent materials lab in the Ironworks. Same faction as Artemis. By Wavelength ecology, two low-spectrum hosts in proximity triggers same-spectrum competition. Morgan's drive to eliminate Artemis wasn't commanded. It was instinct the Wavelengths had been cultivating in Morgan for years — amplified far beyond what the tactical situation warranted. Morgan made the choice. But the choice was made with a mind that had been tuned to perceive low-spectrum rivals as existential threats.

Artemis understood what was happening in the moment before she died — or understood enough. Instead of letting her energy transfer to Nightfall as the ecology intended, she pushed it to Jason.

No host had ever done that before.

Jason now carries both High Spectrum and Low Spectrum energy in a single host. He was never designed for this. The instability he experiences is not his body failing to contain too much power — it is two factions' energies grinding against each other without the second host they were designed to require. He is trying to be the pair alone. The low-spectrum energy inside him is not fully bonded. It is searching for a proper anchor. It is, in the only sense that remains, still Artemis — and it will not rest indefinitely.

**He is not dying because he is too weak.** He is dying because he is holding something that belongs somewhere else, and he has not yet been willing to let it go.

---

## The Path Forward

The Wavelengths' logic reasserts itself regardless of human choice. The low-spectrum energy Jason carries needs a proper low-spectrum host. Morgan Vale is the natural recipient — the low-spectrum host who was present at Artemis's death, who the ecology already pointed toward this energy once.

The difference this time: it would not be a kill. It would be a choice.

Jason releasing Artemis's energy to Morgan is the only path that stabilizes his situation. It requires two things he does not currently have: understanding of what he actually is, and willingness to let go of the last thing he is holding that still feels like her.

Whether he gets there — and what it costs — is the arc.

---

## What the Wavelengths Want Now

Neither faction can directly touch Jason. Their own energy is inside him, and any forced extraction risks destabilizing their foothold entirely. He is outside the system. Both factions are watching. Neither has a category for what he might become if the two energies inside him ever stop fighting and start resolving.

That resolution — if it happens — would not produce a more powerful host. It would produce something the Wavelengths have never encountered: a host who holds both ends of the spectrum in genuine equilibrium. Not a weapon. Not a singularity. Something that answers to neither faction's design.

Whether that is survival or transformation or something else entirely is a question the arc has not yet answered.

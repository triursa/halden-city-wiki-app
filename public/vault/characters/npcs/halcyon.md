## Background

Halcyon formed in Riverside Ward eleven years ago, three teenagers in Priya's parents' living room. Their first EP was recorded above a sandwich shop on the same block where Elena Marquez teaches physics. They are the city's success story in the way cities need success stories: specific, local, impossible to replicate.

Their sound has shifted across four albums — from stripped-down bedroom pop to the layered, orchestral-adjacent production of their most recent record, *Crown & Coast*, which charted nationally and brought them back to Halden City for a homecoming show at Monument Circle that drew forty thousand people.

They name-check Riverside Ward in interviews. Solène does charity work in the neighborhood. The city is proud of them in the particular way that Halden is proud of anything — not worship, just a quiet acknowledgment that this is what it looks like when something makes it out without forgetting where it started.

---

## Story Utility

Halcyon is background. Their presence in the world does a specific thing: they give Halden City a face that isn't superheroes or organized crime. A show, a benefit concert, an interview where someone asks Solène about growing up in Riverside during the Vega Incident — these are the textures that make the city feel inhabited.

If a scene needs a public event with mass presence and no obvious villain angle, a Halcyon concert or promotional appearance is the most natural anchor. Monument Circle show, Crownpoint promotional appearance, school benefit in Riverside — their orbit is wide.

They are not plot-relevant. That is the point.

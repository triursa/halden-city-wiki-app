## Villain Moves

- Deploy Eclipse drone assets through a layer of deniable contractors
- Call in a political favor that makes the heroes' lives structurally harder
- Give a speech that sounds completely reasonable and is completely damning
- Show up at the gala, shake hands with the people hunting him, and mean it
- Fund the next phase of Eclipse biotech operations without ever being in the same room as them

---

## Description

Casimir Vane is charming and he knows it. He is also the money behind the operation, which means he is the least directly useful person in a fight and the most dangerous person in the city. His philanthropy is not performance — he genuinely believes he is in the city's corner. That sincerity is what makes him dangerous. He is not lying when he says he cares. He just cares about a version of the city that requires the current one to be dismantled.

---

## Arc Scope — "Who Decides"

Vane is the funding mechanism. His arc-scope role is Eclipse connection and drone/biotech operations. The Gala is his most exposed moment — enough to implicate, not enough to end him.

**He does not get arrested or fully exposed in this arc.** The deeper question of his institutional backing is the next-arc payoff.

---

## Long-Term Notes

- Who or what is actually backing Vane Capital's growth is unresolved and intentionally undocumented
- Possible connections to A.E.G.I.S., Eclipse, or a third party — do not confirm in "Who Decides"
- His sincerity about the city is not a mask — it complicates any simple villain read and should stay that way
- The Gala exposure is partial: players learn enough to know he's dirty, not enough to prove it cleanly

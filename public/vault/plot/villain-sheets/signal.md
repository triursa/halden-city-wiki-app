## Drive

To prove that heroism is a power structure like any other, and watch it collapse under its own weight.

**Modern** — cold, ideological, constructed. A name that sounds like infrastructure.

## Villain Moves

- Broadcast a hero's worst moment to a live audience
- Engineer a moral dilemma and activate it publicly, then step back and let it play out
- Place Carter Williams in the path of the PCs and let the collision happen
- Surface a piece of the Riley/Carter recording at precisely the wrong moment
- Disappear mid-scene, leaving only a question behind

---

## Description

Signal is not in the room when things go wrong. He is the reason they go wrong. An ordinary human now — no Force, no powers — operating entirely on preparation, patience, and the knowledge that the team will make mistakes he can film. He never sounds angry. He never raises his voice. He places a question and lets it grow.

---

## Arc Scope — "Who Decides"

Signal is the arc's ideological architect. His primary asset is the hospital recording of Riley threatening Carter Williams. His plan is a coordinated public exposure event timed to maximum damage: Signal releases the Riley/Carter footage as a counter-punch to the Gala exposure, deploys a coordinated crisis in Riverside Ward simultaneously, and forces the players to respond publicly and messily.

**He escapes.** The arc does not resolve him.

**Worst-case state:** Supers officially outlawed. Next arc frames the PCs as vigilantes. That outcome is Signal's masterwork — and he is satisfied with it.
